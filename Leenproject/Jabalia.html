What Are Panic Attacks

  • A panic attack causes sudden, temporary feelings of fear and strong physical reactions, nonthreatening situations. When you’re having a panic attack, you may sweat a lot, have difficulty breathing and feel like your heart’s racing. It may feel like you’re having a heart attack.

Sign and Symptoms of a Panic Attack:

  • pounding or racing heartbeat.
  • feeling faint, dizzy or light-headed.
  • sweating, trembling or shaking
  • Panic attacks usually 5 to 20 minutes.
  • Shortness of breath
  • Numbness or tingling
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • feeling disconnected from your body

Reasons (causes) for a panic Attacks

Stress

High levels of stress from work, relationships, or life changes.

Anxiety disorders

Generalized anxiety, social anxiety, or other related conditions.

Trauma

Past traumatic experiences or PTSD.

Phobias

Being exposed to a specific fear (like flying, heights, etc.).

Substance use

Caffeine, drugs, or alcohol can trigger panic attacks.
